# Record What You Learn

Source: Dave Hoover and Adewale Oshineye, *Apprenticeship Patterns* (2009) — provenance: author-verified

## When it applies

- An insight emerges mid-session ("we just used X — that's a real pattern") and is about to evaporate
- A recurring move in the work looks like it might be a named thing
- A hard task ends with something absorbed but not yet articulable

## The rule

Capture insights as they happen — capture is cheap, formalization comes later. A capture needs a name, a one-line gist, and a candidate source; the fuzzy gist is data, not a reason to skip. Capture is not curation: it's a commitment to *consider*, not to formalize. Review at a regular cadence and resolve every entry — promote, fold, defer, or reject. Rejection with a one-line reason is a successful outcome; it maps your taste.

## Anti-patterns

- **Write-only inbox** — capturing forever, reviewing never; the discipline is the cycle
- **Premature formalization** — skipping capture for a full write-up; most insights need to sit and be re-encountered first
- **Perfectionist gist** — refusing to capture until it's crisp
- **Capture as commitment** — treating an entry as a vow instead of an observation

## In the agentic era

The agent sees the pattern forming before the human names it — when the human applies something that matches no recorded concept, surface the capture offer ("this looks like a real pattern — want to record it?") but never auto-capture; the offer is the job, the judgment is theirs. When the human resolves an entry, execute their resolution faithfully — no second-guessing, no promoting anyway.
