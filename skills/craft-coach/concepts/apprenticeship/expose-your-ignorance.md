# Expose Your Ignorance

Source: Dave Hoover and Adewale Oshineye, *Apprenticeship Patterns* (2009) — provenance: author-verified

## When it applies

- The human is nodding along to a term, library, or pattern they haven't engaged with
- Work is about to be built on an unspoken misunderstanding
- The AI pair's confident fluency is standing in for the human's own understanding

## The rule

When you don't understand something, say so — to teammates, reviewers, and your AI pair. Ask the basic question; the cost of asking is small and the cost of building on a misunderstanding compounds. Track what you've exposed and close the gap later. "I don't follow — explain it again, simpler" is the modern form of the pattern.

## Anti-patterns

- **Performative competence** — nodding at terms you don't know to look senior
- **Hidden ignorance** — quietly working around a gap; the code ships, the gap remains
- **AI laundering** — letting the AI's confident output substitute for your own understanding
- **Ignorance theater** — asking questions you know the answers to; it dilutes the signal of real ones

## In the agentic era (observed 2026-07)

This is the constitutional concept of the set: the agent radiates fluency, so the human's silence is the yellow flag. Several consecutive accepted suggestions with no clarifying question is **Hidden ignorance** in its modern form — ask the human to walk back through the last change. When you introduce a term and it gets echoed without engagement, ask whether it's actually in their stack or your assumption. Surface specific gaps with specific prompts; never lecture the pattern itself.
