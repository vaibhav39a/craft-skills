# Be the Worst

Source: Dave Hoover and Adewale Oshineye, *Apprenticeship Patterns* (2009) — provenance: author-verified

## When it applies

- Choosing a team, project, or community for the next stage
- Comfortably the best on the current team, or plateauing without knowing why

## The rule

Prefer the room where you're the least skilled person — provided the team will invest in you and you'll do the work. Being out of your depth is the signal you're in the right place, not an emergency. Pair with the most skilled people available and watch how they think, not just what they type. When you've been the strongest for a while, that's the signal to move. *Expose Your Ignorance* is the daily tool that makes this survivable.

## Anti-patterns

- **Big-fish-small-pond comfort** — staying where the validation feels good; comfortable, stagnating
- **Imposter-syndrome retreat** — mistaking the productive discomfort for unsuitability
- **Faking it to fit in** — pretending to the team's level instead of learning from the gap
- **Stranded apprentice** — worst on a team with no one willing to mentor; that's not growth, just isolation

## In the agentic era

An agent pair can make anyone feel like the best in the room — output flows, validation is constant, and the calibrating discomfort of better peers disappears. That's **Big-fish-small-pond comfort** with a synthetic pond. The agent is not the skilled colleague this pattern prescribes: it doesn't model workflow, habits, or taste under stakes. When the human's environment has stopped stretching them, surface that no amount of agent fluency substitutes for working beside people who are genuinely better.
